Manicotti

My favorite Italian dish!

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • 1 box manicotti shells
  • 3 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

For the sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 basil sprig
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Pinch of sugar (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes

Instructions

Prepare sauce

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add minced garlic and chopped onion, sauté until softened and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add the basil sprig and dried oregano, stirring to combine.
  3. Pour in the white wine, scraping the bottom of the skillet to deglaze and incorporate any browned bits. Let it simmer for 2-3 minutes until the wine reduces slightly.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, salt, black pepper, and a pinch of sugar if desired. Simmer the sauce for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and flavors meld together. Remove the basil sprig before using.

Prepare filling and Shells

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese, shredded mozzarella, grated\ Parmesan cheese, egg, minced garlic, chopped parsley, dried basil, salt, and black pepper. Mix well.
  2. Cook manicotti shells according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and let them cool slightly before handling.
  3. Using a spoon or piping bag, fill each manicotti shell with the cheese mixture, being careful not to overfill.
  4. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

Assemble and Bake

  1. Spread a thin layer of the prepared tomato sauce on the bottom of a baking dish to prevent sticking.
  2. Arrange the filled manicotti shells in a single layer in the baking dish.
  3. Pour the remaining tomato sauce evenly over the filled shells, ensuring they are well covered.
  4. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the oven for 25-30 minutes, until the sauce is bubbly and the cheese filling is heated through.
