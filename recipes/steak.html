Steak

My favorite way to cook steak is to sear it in a hot cast iron skillet and finish it in the oven. Here's how I do it:

Ingredients

  • 1 thick-cut steak (ribeye, strip, or filet mignon)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or canola oil
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2-3 sprigs of fresh thyme or rosemary (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Pat the steak dry with paper towels. Season generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper on both sides.
  3. Heat a cast iron skillet over high heat until it's smoking hot. Add the vegetable oil and swirl to coat the bottom of the skillet.
  4. Carefully place the steak in the skillet and sear for 2-3 minutes without moving it, until a deep brown crust forms. Flip the steak and sear the other side for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add the butter, smashed garlic, and fresh herbs (if using) to the skillet. Tilt the skillet slightly and use a spoon to baste the steak with the melted butter and aromatics for about 30 seconds.
  6. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and roast for 5-7 minutes for medium-rare, or until it reaches your desired level of doneness. Use a meat thermometer to check: 130°F (54°C) for medium-rare, 140°F (60°C) for medium.
  7. Remove the steak from the oven and let it rest on a cutting board for 5-10 minutes before slicing. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat.
  8. Slice against the grain and serve with your favorite sides. Enjoy!
